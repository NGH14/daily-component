Bun Bo

Bun Bo Hue is a Vietnamese noodle soup originating from the city of Huế, known for its spicy, savory, and slightly sweet broth with a distinct lemongrass flavor. It's typically made with rice vermicelli noodles (bún), beef, and sometimes pork knuckles, crab cakes, and pork blood pudding. The dish is a staple of Central Vietnamese cuisine
