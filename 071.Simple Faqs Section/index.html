Bún Bò FAQs

Bún Bò is a popular Vietnamese rice noodle soup that originated from the city of Huế in central Vietnam. The dish features thin rice noodles (bún) in a flavorful lemongrass beef broth with tender slices of beef and sometimes pork. It's known for its balance of spicy, sour, sweet, and savory flavors.

The full name "Bún Bò Huế" refers to its city of origin, though it's often shortened to just "Bún Bò" in many parts of Vietnam.

The main ingredients in Bún Bò include:

  • Rice noodles (bún): Round, medium-thickness rice noodles
  • Beef: Usually beef shank, sometimes oxtail or other cuts
  • Pork: Often includes cubes of congealed pig's blood and/or pork hock
  • Lemongrass: Essential for the broth's distinctive flavor
  • Shrimp paste (mắm ruốc): Gives the broth its umami depth
  • Annatto seeds/oil: Provides the broth's reddish color
  • Herbs and vegetables: Bean sprouts, lime, Vietnamese coriander, mint, sliced banana flower, red cabbage
  • Chili paste/oil: For the signature spiciness

Yes, Bún Bò is traditionally spicy. The spiciness comes from both chili oil or paste in the broth and from fresh chilies served as a garnish. The level of spiciness can vary significantly from restaurant to restaurant.

When ordering, you can typically request your preferred spice level. In Vietnamese restaurants outside Vietnam, the dish might be served milder by default, with chili sauce on the side to adjust to your taste.

To enjoy Bún Bò the traditional way:

  1. Add fresh herbs, bean sprouts, and lime juice to your bowl according to taste
  2. Mix in some chili sauce or oil if you want it spicier
  3. Try to get a bit of everything in each bite - noodles, meat, and herbs

Some people prefer to add fish sauce or hoisin sauce, though purists might consider this unnecessary as the broth is already very flavorful.
